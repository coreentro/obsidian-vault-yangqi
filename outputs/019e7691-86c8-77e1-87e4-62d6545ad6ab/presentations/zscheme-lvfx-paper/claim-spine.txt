thesis:
The paper improves photocatalysis by organizing three material roles around a practical contradiction: stronger charge separation without sacrificing redox power, plus magnetic recovery.

audience:
research group meeting

one-line arc:
problem -> material division of labor -> synthesis -> proof of performance -> proof of mechanism -> application boundary -> lessons for our experiments

slides:
01 A three-component design turns activity, light harvesting and recovery into one system.
02 The paper solves four practical contradictions through material division of labor.
03 This is a surface-decorated heterojunction, not a strict continuous core-shell particle.
04 A mild deposition-precipitation step assembles the ternary catalyst after preparing NCDs and ZnFe2O4.
05 Under visible light, the ternary sample reaches 88.75% LVFX degradation in 90 minutes.
06 The response range broadens to NIR, but the performance boundary remains visible.
07 Charge-separation evidence, not surface area, explains the activity gain.
08 The Z-scheme retains the strongest redox sites and generates three active species.
09 Magnetic recovery is promising, while four limitations still matter for practical claims.
10 The reusable lesson is an evidence chain for designing our next degradation experiment.
